I am a B.S. student in Statistics (Minor in Mathematics) at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, graduating in 2026. I think of myself as a junior AI researcher working at the intersection of statistics, machine learning, and AI safety.

My central interest is **AI safety and alignment**—making large language models and agentic systems more robust, honest, and controllable. I have reproduced and studied a range of alignment methods, including SecAlign (prompt-injection defense), Circuit Breakers (representation engineering), Safe-RLHF, and Latent Adversarial Training, and I read closely in agent safety, multi-turn risk, and code-agent security. Alongside this, I work on causal inference and causal ML, Bayesian modeling and optimization, and graph machine learning.

What I rely on most is **cross-domain transfer**—an instinct for connecting ideas that look unrelated. I tend to take a research framework apart functionally, ask what each component does and where it transfers, run a small experiment that alters the causal direction, and then combine complementary streams into a single working loop. That habit has carried across counterfactual climate-risk modeling at City University of Hong Kong, a memory system for a mental-health support agent at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, and AI/software engineering internships at NetEase Youdao, PhinD, and in finance (graph-based fraud detection).

Before statistics and computer science, I studied the social sciences—and I think of that training as a gift I carry into the technical work. You can read why in [Another River](/blog/2026/another-river/).
